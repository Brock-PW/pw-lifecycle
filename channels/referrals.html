AI-First Referral Architecture

ClaudeBrock / team — approvers
Claude manages all sequence timing, drafts all outreach, monitors for new Google reviews, and flags referral credit events to Rachel. Every email is drafted by Claude and approved by Brock before send. The website testimonial outreach is the only exception — that is handled personally by Rachel, Megan, or Anthy because those relationships require a human touch.

Trigger: Project Closeout in Zoho

Zoho triggerClaude
The entire referral and review sequence is triggered by the Project Closeout stage being confirmed in Zoho. Claude logs the trigger date and all subsequent timing is calculated relative to Day 0. No manual scheduling needed — the sequence fires automatically.

Day 0 — Tag as Referral Eligible

ClaudeAutomated
Claude tags the client record in Zoho as "Referral Eligible" and logs the closeout date. Activates the referral tracking fields. HOOK TO BUILD: Zoho fields needed — "Referred By" (lookup to referring client record) and "Referral Credit Applied Date." Rachel will apply the credit manually until automation is built.

Day 1 — Thank You + Google Review Ask

Claude draftsBrock approves
Sent Day 1 after closeout. Claude drafts in Brock's voice — warm, personal, genuine. Includes a direct link to the PW Google Business Profile review page. One send only — never ask twice for a Google review. No mention of the referral program in this email. This moment is purely about thanking the client and the relationship.

Day 7 — Referral Program Introduction

Claude draftsBrock approves
$250 bill credit applied to the referring client's next invoice once their referred client pays their first invoice. Claude drafts the email — casual, not salesy. "If you know anyone else doing public works in California who could use help with compliance, we'd love the introduction. We take good care of anyone you send our way — and we'll apply a $250 credit to your next invoice as a thank you."

Referral Credit Logic

RachelClaude — flags
HOOK TO BUILD: when a new client is onboarded, Zoho should check if they were referred by an existing client. If yes, Claude flags Rachel when that new client pays their first invoice. Rachel manually applies the $250 credit to the referring client's next invoice and logs the credit date in both Zoho records. Day 7 — Website Testimonial Ask

Rachel / Megan / Anthy
This is not AI-drafted. Rachel, Megan, or Anthy personally reaches out to clients they have a strong relationship with. The ask: a written testimonial for the PW website — 2-3 sentences about their experience. Not every client gets asked — team identifies the right ones based on relationship quality, project complexity, and how articulate the client is. Completely separate from the Google review ask.

Day 7 Cap — Handoff to Retention

Stage 5 Retention
The referral channel sequence ends at Day 7. From here, ongoing client touches (Day 30 check-in, Day 90 quarterly touch, annual renewal nudge) are managed in Stage 5 — Retention. This keeps the referral channel focused and prevents it from becoming a long-running nurture sequence.

Existing Clients Post-Closeout

Brock / team
Primary referral source. Satisfaction is highest immediately after a smooth project close. The Day 7 email catches them at exactly the right moment. Best referrers tend to be clients who had a complex project — they appreciate the work the most and have the most credible story to tell.

GCs Referring Their Subs

Brock / Anthy
GCs are jointly liable for subcontractor noncompliance. When a GC refers their subs to PW, they're protecting themselves as much as helping the sub. Frame the referral ask to GCs around this angle: "We can take compliance off your plate for the whole project — not just your portion." Track GC-to-sub referrals separately in Zoho.

Subs Referring Peer Contractors

Brock / Anthy
Trade network referrals. Peer trust is very high in the construction industry — contractors talk to each other constantly. "We use Prevailing Wisdom on all our jobs" is the most powerful marketing PW has. Track in Zoho as source "Peer Referral" + note the referring contractor.
