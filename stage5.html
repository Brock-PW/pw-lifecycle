Client Confirms → Stage 4 Active

Zoho trigger
Client confirms a project is starting. Status immediately moves to Active. Zoho Projects task list is created. Stage 4 restarts. All history from Stage 5 is preserved in the Notion client brain and informs the new project setup.

Status: Warm

ZohoClaude
No active project. No known upcoming work. Within 60 days of the last project close. This is a normal state for any active client between projects. The goal is to stay present without being pushy — warm, not salesy.

Day 30 — Check-In

Claude draftsAM reviews + sends
Sent 30 days after the last project closes. Claude drafts a warm, specific message that references the last project by name — never a generic "checking in" email. "How did the Ritchen project wrap up for you?" AM reviews and sends. This is a human touch — it needs to feel personal.

Claude Watches for Signals

Claude
Between touchpoints Claude continuously watches: new DIR registrations for this contractor, PWCR renewal coming up, rate changes that affect their work, any inbound email or Otter mention. When a signal fires, it surfaces immediately in the dashboard as an action item.

Client Responds — Status Reset

ClaudeZoho
Client responds at any point. Claude reads the response, identifies intent, and resets status accordingly. New work confirmed: Job Pending. Project starting now: Active. Just checking in: stays Warm. Zoho and Notion updated automatically.

Day 60 — Status Check

ClaudeAutomated
60 days with no response or meaningful engagement. Claude checks whether any touch has been acknowledged. If yes — status stays and the right path continues. If no — status moves to Recovery and a different approach begins.

Status: Recovery

ZohoClaude
60 days with no response. Status moves to Recovery. The relationship is not dead — but it needs a different approach. The goal shifts from staying present to giving them a genuine reason to respond. Tone: "we're here when you need us."

Recovery Sequence — AI Manages

Claude
Claude manages the recovery sequence automatically. Every touch is specific to this client — not generic marketing. Uses real compliance signals: PWCR expiring, rate changes in their county, DIR activity in their area. "Your PWCR expires June 30 — just a heads up in case you have anything coming up." Claude drafts and sends.

Human Escalation Touch

Brock / AMClaude drafts
After 2 automated recovery touches with no response, Claude surfaces the client to the dashboard for personal outreach. Short. Direct. No pressure. "Hey Brian — just wanted to make sure you know we're still here." Claude drafts using the Notion client brain. Brock or AM reviews and sends personally.

Long-Term Dormant

Claude
No response to the human escalation touch. Client moves to long-term dormant. Claude maintains a very low-cadence presence — PWCR renewal blast annually, rate change alerts when directly relevant. Never fully abandon. A client who went cold can come back in 18 months. The Notion client brain has everything when they do.

Client Re-Engaged — Status Reset

ClaudeZoho
Client responds. Claude reads the response and determines the right status reset. Job Pending if they have work coming. Active if a project is starting. Warm if they're just reconnecting. Zoho and Notion updated. The right path resumes from where they re-enter.

→ Job Pending · Warm · or Stage 4 Active

System
Client re-enters the appropriate state. If a new project is confirmed, Stage 4 restarts immediately with all prior history intact. The Notion client brain carries forward everything PW knows about this client.

Trigger: Contract Signed + Retainer Paid

Zoho trigger
The moment contract is signed and retainer is paid in Stage 2, Zoho updates the deal stage to Active Client and Stage 3 fires automatically. Nothing manual. The onboarding sequence begins within minutes of the signature.

Account Manager Assigned

BrockZoho notifies AM
Brock assigns the account manager in Zoho. AM is notified immediately. Before making any contact, the AM reads the Stage 2 Otter transcript and pre-call brief so they walk into the relationship already knowing the client's situation. No cold introductions.

Onboarding Email

Claude draftsAM reviews
Auto-sends within minutes of contract signing. Claude drafts in the AM's voice. AM reviews before it goes out. Warm, personal, confident. Not a template — it references specific things from the consultation call. Sets the tone for the entire relationship.

Onboarding Email Contents

Claude drafts
Introduces the AM by name with a brief bio and photo. Provides direct contact details (email + phone). States response time commitment. Explains clearly what happens next. References something specific from the consultation call to show they were listening. Tone: warm, capable, like a trusted expert they're lucky to have.

Bobby / Brock

"Working With PW" Document

Claude producesAM reviews
A short, clean document that sets the tone for how the relationship works. Not a legal document. Not a compliance guide. A communication guide. Claude produces a personalised version for each client. AM reviews before sending. Should feel like a welcome letter from a trusted partner, not an onboarding packet from a vendor.

"Working With PW" — Contents

ClaudeAM personalises
Covers: who we are and what we believe in · meet your AM · how we communicate (preferred channels, response times) · what we need from you to do our best work · how to flag an issue or urgent situation · what great looks like when we work well together · one sentence on what happens next. No project details. No compliance specifics. Pure relationship setup.

AM Introduction Call

AMOtter records
AM reaches out within 48 hours of onboarding email to schedule a short 15-20 min call. Optional but strongly encouraged. Goal: put a voice to the name, answer any early questions, build rapport before the work begins. Explicitly not a project call — if project questions come up, note them and schedule a separate call. Otter records. Claude extracts key points → Notion client brain.

Onboarding Complete

AM confirms
Client has: met their AM, received the welcome kit, received the Working With PW document, and had an intro call if they wanted one. Notion brain is live. Communication expectations are set. Stage 3 is done. The relationship is established. Now we wait for the first project.

→ Stage 4: Active Project Loop

Trigger: first project starts
Stage 4 begins when the client's first project starts — not when onboarding completes. A client could be in Stage 3 (onboarded, no active project) for days or weeks before Stage 4 begins. That gap is managed through Stage 5 retention touchpoints until a project kicks off.
