Entry: Client Explicitly Requests Termination

BrockZoho
This stage is entered only when a client explicitly asks to end the relationship or stop receiving contact. It is NOT for cold clients — cold clients belong in Stage 5 Recovery. It is NOT for clients between projects — those belong in Stage 5 Warm or Job Pending. Zoho status is updated immediately when termination is confirmed.

Leave the Door Open?

Brock
Brock makes this call based on the nature of the exit. Most terminations should be "Follow-Up" — the door stays open and we check in after 12 months. "No Contact" is reserved for situations where a client explicitly does not want to hear from PW again, or where the relationship ended badly enough that outreach would be unwelcome.

Status: Terminated — Follow-Up

ZohoClaude
Client is done for now but the door is not closed. Maybe they are pausing public works work, switching to a competitor, or the relationship just ran its course. Status set in Zoho. No contact for 12 months. Notion client brain preserved in full.

12-Month Silence

Claude
Claude sets a 12-month timer and goes quiet. Zero automated touches. Zero outreach. No PWCR reminders. No rate alerts. Nothing. The Notion client brain stays intact — it just sits there, waiting. When 12 months passes, Claude surfaces the account to the dashboard. ⚠ HOOK/CLAUDE: timer + surface to dashboard after 12 months.

12-Month Check-In — Human Sends

Brock / AMClaude drafts
One single low-key check-in after 12 months. Claude drafts it using the Notion client brain — referencing something real, something personal if possible. Short. No pressure. No sales pitch. "Just wanted to see how things are going." Brock or AM reviews and sends personally. If no response — stays in Stage 6. If positive response — re-enters Stage 5.

Client Responds → Re-enter Stage 5

ZohoClaude
Positive response at any point — from the 12-month check-in or any unprompted contact. Client re-enters Stage 5 as Warm (reconnecting, no project yet) or Job Pending (new work coming). All Notion history is carried forward. The relationship resumes from where it left off, with full context intact.

Status: Terminated — No Contact

ZohoClaude
Reserved for situations where a client explicitly requests no further contact, or where the relationship ended badly enough that outreach would be unwelcome. Status flagged in both Zoho and the Notion client brain. Claude prevents any automated outreach from firing for this account.

No Automated Touches — Ever

Claude
Claude flag on the account prevents all automated outreach. No PWCR reminders. No rate change alerts. No recovery sequence. No anniversary checks. No 12-month check-in. The account is dark. The only way it re-opens is if the client contacts PW themselves.

Client Initiates Contact

Brock
If the client reaches out to PW unprompted — email, phone call, referral — the account is surfaced to Brock immediately. Brock decides whether to re-engage. This decision is never automated. Brock makes the call based on his knowledge of what happened and his judgment about the relationship.

Brock Decides → Re-engage or Maintain

Brock
Re-engage: Brock approves. Status updated in Zoho. Client re-enters Stage 5. Notion brain re-activated. Relationship resumes. Maintain: Brock decides not to respond or to respond briefly without re-opening the account. Status stays. No automated response is sent without Brock's explicit approval.

Notion Client Brain — Always Preserved

Claude
The Notion client brain page is never deleted, not even for Do Not Contact accounts. If a terminated client returns after two years, everything PW knows about them is still there — every project, every relationship note, every interaction. The brain is the institutional memory of the relationship. It outlasts any status change.

Trigger: Contract Signed + Retainer Paid

Zoho trigger
The moment contract is signed and retainer is paid in Stage 2, Zoho updates the deal stage to Active Client and Stage 3 fires automatically. Nothing manual. The onboarding sequence begins within minutes of the signature.

Account Manager Assigned

BrockZoho notifies AM
Brock assigns the account manager in Zoho. AM is notified immediately. Before making any contact, the AM reads the Stage 2 Otter transcript and pre-call brief so they walk into the relationship already knowing the client's situation. No cold introductions.

Onboarding Email

Claude draftsAM reviews
Auto-sends within minutes of contract signing. Claude drafts in the AM's voice. AM reviews before it goes out. Warm, personal, confident. Not a template — it references specific things from the consultation call. Sets the tone for the entire relationship.

Onboarding Email Contents

Claude drafts
Introduces the AM by name with a brief bio and photo. Provides direct contact details (email + phone). States response time commitment. Explains clearly what happens next. References something specific from the consultation call to show they were listening. Tone: warm, capable, like a trusted expert they're lucky to have.

Bobby / Brock

"Working With PW" Document

Claude producesAM reviews
A short, clean document that sets the tone for how the relationship works. Not a legal document. Not a compliance guide. A communication guide. Claude produces a personalised version for each client. AM reviews before sending. Should feel like a welcome letter from a trusted partner, not an onboarding packet from a vendor.

"Working With PW" — Contents

ClaudeAM personalises
Covers: who we are and what we believe in · meet your AM · how we communicate (preferred channels, response times) · what we need from you to do our best work · how to flag an issue or urgent situation · what great looks like when we work well together · one sentence on what happens next. No project details. No compliance specifics. Pure relationship setup.

AM Introduction Call

AMOtter records
AM reaches out within 48 hours of onboarding email to schedule a short 15-20 min call. Optional but strongly encouraged. Goal: put a voice to the name, answer any early questions, build rapport before the work begins. Explicitly not a project call — if project questions come up, note them and schedule a separate call. Otter records. Claude extracts key points → Notion client brain.

Onboarding Complete

AM confirms
Client has: met their AM, received the welcome kit, received the Working With PW document, and had an intro call if they wanted one. Notion brain is live. Communication expectations are set. Stage 3 is done. The relationship is established. Now we wait for the first project.

→ Stage 4: Active Project Loop

Trigger: first project starts
Stage 4 begins when the client's first project starts — not when onboarding completes. A client could be in Stage 3 (onboarded, no active project) for days or weeks before Stage 4 begins. That gap is managed through Stage 5 retention touchpoints until a project kicks off.
